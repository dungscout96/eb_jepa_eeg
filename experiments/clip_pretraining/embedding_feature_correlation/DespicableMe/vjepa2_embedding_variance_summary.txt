CLIP design implications:
  * CENTER/WHITEN BEFORE CONTRASTING. Raw cosine AUC=0.90 with everything
    near 1.0; after mean-centering AUC=0.91. The discriminative
    signal lives in a small off-mean component, so either subtract the
    global mean / batch-norm the targets, or rely on a learnable
    temperature small enough to expand that range.
  * Strong shot structure (eta^2=0.80, centered AUC=0.91): same-shot
    clips are reliably closer than cross-shot ones. Shot-level positives
    (clip<->shot-mean, or two clips from one shot) are well-posed and
    cross-shot pairs are mostly clean negatives.
  * Temporally adjacent clips (gap<~2s) are very similar even ACROSS shot
    boundaries (see [3]) -> exclude near-in-time clips from the negative
    pool (false negatives), and beware that 'same-shot positive' is partly
    just 'nearby in time'.
  * top-1 NN purity 0.91: ~9% of clips' nearest neighbor is in ANOTHER shot
    -> in-batch negatives include genuinely hard (near-duplicate) cases;
    a moderate temperature + many negatives will help more than mining.
