CLIP design implications (CLIP-encoder space):
  * Centering helps (AUC 0.92 -> 0.95). CLIP
    embeddings carry a non-trivial shared component, similar to V-JEPA-2.
  * Strong shot structure (eta^2=0.71, centered AUC=0.95): same-shot
    frames are reliably closer than cross-shot ones; shot-level positives
    are well-posed.
  * At 24 Hz, ADJACENT FRAMES are nearly identical even across shot cuts;
    exclude tight-time-gap pairs from the negative pool, or sub-sample to
    ~1-2 Hz before contrasting to avoid trivial positives.
  * top-1 NN purity 1.00: ~0% of frames' nearest neighbor lies in another shot.
